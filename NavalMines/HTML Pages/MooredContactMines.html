Moored Contact Mines

Moored Mines are assembled in buoyant cases and held in place by anchors. There are three types of moored mines:

  • close-tethered mines near the sea floor
  • in-volume mines
  • near-surface mines

Moored mines require a large internal air space to allow the mine case to become buoyant, which limits the amount of explosives that they can contain. As a result, the damage radius of a moored mine is generally less than that of a bottom mine. Some moored mines, however, are armed with torpedoes or rockets that greatly increase the weapons' “reach” against submerged and surface targets.
